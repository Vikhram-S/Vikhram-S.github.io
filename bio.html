Biography

Vikhram S is a researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, multimodal reasoning systems, and public-interest technology. His work spans vision–language models, explainable AI, constitutional AI, scientific reasoning systems, and domain-specific intelligence architectures, with applications in healthcare, legal informatics, and technology governance.

His research focuses on the design of AI systems that integrate perception, language, retrieval, and structured reasoning to support decision-making in complex and high-stakes environments. He has developed multimodal AI frameworks for healthcare, including explainable vision–language systems for radiological reasoning, confidence-aware inference pipelines, and interpretable machine learning architectures designed to improve transparency, reliability, and human oversight.

He is the creator of IndianConstitution, an open source constitutional intelligence platform enabling semantic exploration and analysis of India's constitutional texts. The project has achieved more than 31,000 downloads through PyPI and serves as publicly accessible research infrastructure supporting constitutional AI, legal NLP, civic technology, and governance oriented AI applications.

His work has been recognized through publication in the India AI Impact Summit 2026 Compendium, jointly convened by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India, and UN Women, where his research on gender-responsive AI was selected among 23 case studies from more than 235 submissions across 50 countries. His research has also been published in Springer's Lecture Notes in Networks and Systems (LNNS) series, with his work on explainable multimodal AI for healthcare identified in the acceptance notification as among the top 15% of papers received and reviewed at ICICC 2026.

Beyond technical research, he is interested in the broader relationship between artificial intelligence, institutions, and society. His interests increasingly encompass AI governance, technology policy, digital public infrastructure, and the development of trustworthy and socially beneficial AI systems capable of supporting public decision-making and human development.

He currently serves as an invited Advisor to the Machine Learning Tech Society at Saveetha Engineering College, where he mentors student researchers and contributes to advanced technical programs in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and emerging technologies.

He holds a B.E. in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Saveetha Engineering College, India, completed in 2026.

His long-term goal is to contribute to the development of trustworthy, interpretable, and societally impactful AI systems while advancing research at the intersection of machine intelligence, governance, and public-interest innovation.
