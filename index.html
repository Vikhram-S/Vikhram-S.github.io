Research Interests

I am interested in developing robust and interpretable machine learning systems for real-world applications, with a primary focus on multimodal learning and intelligent systems. My research broadly lies at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Computer Vision, and Deep Learning.

My current research can be broadly categorized into the following thematic areas:

Multimodal Learning and Vision--Language Models: Integration of visual and textual modalities for tasks such as image understanding, report generation, and multimodal reasoning, with applications in healthcare and scientific domains.

Explainable and Trustworthy AI: Designing interpretable machine learning models with an emphasis on transparency, reliability, and human-aligned decision-making in high-stakes applications.

Data-Efficient and Scalable Learning: Exploring approaches involving small language models, efficient training strategies, and deployment of machine learning systems under limited data and resource constraints.

Applied AI for Healthcare: Development of machine learning solutions for medical imaging tasks such as radiology and dermatology, focusing on clinically meaningful and deployable systems.
